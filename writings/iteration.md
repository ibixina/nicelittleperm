Plan -> Execute -> Test -> Repeat

a simple four step process that underlies all innovatoin, and invention. it gave us the wheel, agriculture, computers and will be the core of superintelligence. 

humans are often limited at how fast they can iterate. each step of the process takes us minutes to hours to years. so in a typical human lifespan, that is purely the time constraint ignoring the labor constraint and the attention constraint. at most any one human will be able to go through this process a few thousand times. so collectively all of humanity has gone through this process billions of times. so billions of iteration allowed us to go from fire to modern supercomputers. 

the key element to this process has been the doer. doer who has the agency and intelligence to carry out this process end to end. and for the first time in human history, we have the possibility of a doer that is not human: an agent. 

a software agent isn't constrained by any of the human limitations. what it lacks in intelligence, it can compensate for it with brute computation. an agent capable of this end to end cycle can go through entirety of human iteration cycles in weeks. the first digital agent capable of doing all 4 steps will be able to progress at an exponential pace. this will be the emergence of superintelligence.

what are the limitations of a cycle? what happens if you give a dumb model the agency to execute all 4 steps. how far can this progress. can a bad idea iterated thousands of times be better than a good idea or will it just be an garbage dump of band aids. 

today's large language models are the core behind agentic actions. but they are often only good at the execution part of the 4steps. they can write the code. they can mimic competence at the plan step. completely inept at the test step. test step stands as the biggest bottleneck of this process. this is the step where the agents still need to rely on external intervention. 

iterative progress is a process of searching for the optimal solution in a complx latent space. so even if automated search has the capacity to explore and evaluate billions of possible solutions, they are still limited by the space they are in. they can make exponential progress, but they will reach the local optima expoentially quickly. 

iterative progress only works if you can ensure you can always improve upon the current solution in finite time.
