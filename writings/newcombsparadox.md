# 1 box or 2

Premise:

1. A supercomputer/entity capable of predicting whether you can 1 box or both
2. If you take both, the second box will have $0, and if you only take second second box will have $1m
3. The entity predicts what you are going to do, and decides on whether to place $1m in the second box in the past.


two boxes: $1000 or $1m.

the main problem with this premise is our view of freewill.

One may reason that their action in the present cannot influence the decision the super computer made in the past. So, one should always take the 2 boxes.

But this anthropomorphizes the super computer or demon or god. We are projecting our limitation to the predicting entity.

We assume that our present actions can't influence the past, but we miss the fact that our present actions may entirely be dependent on our past state. And such a being may be able to compute the future. So, one should always take the mystery box.

Either it has $1m and you just lose out on $1k extra, or it has nothing and you only lost $1k, but you showed the fallibility of this entity. free will is a question of computation rather than it having any mystical properties.
