there is no task that is hard.

a task can be hard-work, but it is never hard.

hard isn't i tried something and couldn't do it, so it must be hard. hard is i put in the required amount of effort and time in learning it and i still couldn't do it. so, there is no thing that is hard.

people are intimidated by hard things because of our relationship with failure. we see failure as something to avoid. for our entire life, we are taught the value of success, excellence. failure is seen as something to avoid, to be afraid of , to dread. we try to avoid failure, so we never try anything hard. 

failure is a gauge. it is an important skill to fail and to be able to keep going, keep improving. so we must change our relationship with failure, instea of seeing it as something to dread, we should embrace it.

natural ability is highly overstated. there certainly is individual variation but the range isn't from monkey to Prime Intellect, the actual range is your average guy to slightly above average guy. The variation becomes a non-factor.
